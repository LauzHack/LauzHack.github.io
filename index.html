F.A.Q.

What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is an invention marathon. Programmers, designers, builders and more come together to learn, build, and share their creations over the course of a 24 hours.

How much does it cost?

Thanks to our sponsors, attendance is completely free.

How do I get there?

LauzHack takes place at on EPFL's campus in Lausanne, Switzerland. You can find the directions from main public transit locations here. Travel reimbursements might be possible, watch this space!

Where will I sleep?

Hacking all night can get tiring. We'll have a few bean bags in a quiet room for you to nap at the venue. However, it is best to come prepared. Sleep well beforehand and feel free to bring your own sleeping bag. There will also be limited access to showers.

What should I bring??

Bring your laptop, charger and other gadgets you might use for your project. Feel free to bring your pillows, toiletries, headphones, etc.
All attendees must bring a valid ID for admission.

What will I eat?

We'll take care of providing meals at the event from breakfast on Saturday to lunch on Sunday.

Can I attend?

As long as you are a student or have graduated from university in past twelve months, you are eligible to participate.

What if I don't have a team?

Don't worry! Part of the fun of a hackathon is meeting new people at the beginning. We will have time at the beginning of the event for everyone to socialize and form teams of up to 4 people.

What if I have never been to a hackathon?

LauzHack is very beginner friendly, there will be plenty of mentors and a range of workshops to help you out before and during LauzHack.

Do I own my hack?

Yes.
Anything you create will be owned solely by you.

Why should I pre-register?

You will get a priority during registration!
We will also add you to our mailing list to send you important updates.
