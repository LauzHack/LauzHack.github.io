EPFL, Switzerland

Participant applications are now closed

We present the first edition of LauzHack, a student-run hackathon at EPFL in Lausanne, Switzerland. Come and join over 200 like-minded student-hackers hacking together for 24 hours to make something amazing!


So, what's a hackathon? It's a creativity marathon, where attendees work in teams of up to four and put their ideas together to create something exciting in only 24 hours. It gives teams of hackers a chance to turn their ideas into reality.

F.A.Q.

Can I participate?

You must be at least 18 years old and a student, or have graduated from university in the past twelve months.

How much does it cost?

Thanks to our sponsors, attendance is completely free for all accepted hackers. We will provide you with WiFi, meals, swag, and a workspace.

What should I bring?

Bring your laptop, charger and other gadgets you might use for your project.
Please bring a valid ID for admission.

Where will I sleep?

We have a sleeping area with mattresses, and showers.
Please bring your sleeping bag, towel, and toiletries.
We can arrange a place for you to sleep on Friday night too.

Where is LauzHack being held?

LauzHack will take place in EPFL's Lausanne campus. It's located in the southwestern part of Switzerland, bordering Lake Geneva. You can find directions here.

Will I get travel reimbursement?

We will be providing partial travel scholarships to participants upto a maximum of 100CHF.
Please include your request when confirming your participation.

What about intellectual property?

We don't claim any rights on your Intellectual property or the intellectual property generated by you during the hackathon or outside the hackathon. Everything you create belongs to you.

What if I don't have a team?

Don't worry! Part of the fun of a hackathon is meeting new people. We will have time at the beginning of the event to socialize and form teams of up to 4.
